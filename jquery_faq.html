10 FAQ of Parks

1.How old is the National Park System?
The National Park Service was created by an act signed by President Woodrow Wilson on August 25, 1916.
2.How many people visit the national parks?
Total recreation visitors to the national parks in 2018: 318,211,833.
3.Can i bring my pet to a national park?
Some national parks welcome pets—in developed areas, on many trails and campgrounds, and in some lodging facilities
4. Can i bring my service dog to a national park?
Yes, service dogs are legally permitted anywhere in a park that visitors can go
5. Who is the director of the national parks?
Dan Smith
6. What government agency oversees the national park service?
The National Park Service is a bureau of the Department of the Interior. Directly overseeing its operation is the department's Assistant Secretary for Fish, Wildlife and Parks.
7.How many employees are in the national park service?
More than 20,000
8. How many people volunteer?
More than 315,000
9. How many areas are in the national park system?
419
10. Whats the largest national park site?
Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and Preserve, AK at 13.2 million acres.

Facts about some of the parks:

Zion National Park

  • Before it was a national park, Zion was a national monument, and it’s name wasn’t Zion.
  • Zion is home to one of the greatest engineering feats of modern times.
  • Zion’s Angels Landing provides spectacular views, but it’s one of the world’s most dangerous.
  • Zion Canyon is continually changing.

Arches National Park

  • It contains the highest density of natural arches in the world.
  • About 300 million years ago an inland sea covered what is now Arches National Park. The sea evaporated and re-formed 29 times in all, leaving behind salt beds thousands of feet thick.
  • There are more than 2000 natural sandstone arches located in Arches National Park, which is the inspiration for the park's name.
  • Delicate Arch is the most famous and recognized arch in Arches National Park. It is also a symbol of the state of Utah.

Sequoia National Park

  • Much of the population of Native American people that lived in the region when the European settlers arrived was wiped out because of smallpox.
  • The most massive tree in the world - the General Sherman Tree - is located in the Giant Forest in Sequoia National Park.
  • The majority of Sequoia National Park, much like Kings Canyon National Park, is only accessible on horseback or on foot.
  • As many as 1.5 million people visit Sequoia National Park each year.
